Essential information on what to do before, during, and after an earthquake to protect yourself and your loved ones.

üõ°Ô∏è Before Earthquake: Preparation

üìù Create an Emergency Plan

  • Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy furniture, against interior walls)
  • Establish family meeting places near your home and outside your neighborhood
  • Choose an out-of-town contact person everyone can call to check in
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" drills with all household members
  • Plan for family members with special needs, including pets

üéí Build an Emergency Kit

  • Water: At least 1 gallon per person per day for 3 days
  • Food: 3-day supply of non-perishable items
  • First aid kit: Bandages, antiseptics, medications, prescriptions
  • Tools & Supplies: Flashlight, batteries, radio, whistle, multi-tool
  • Important documents: Copies of IDs, insurance, medical records in waterproof container
  • Cash: Small bills and coins (ATMs may not work)
  • Sanitation items: Toilet paper, garbage bags, personal hygiene products

üè† Earthquake-Proof Your Home

  • Secure heavy furniture, appliances, and water heaters to wall studs
  • Install latches on cabinet doors and use safety film on windows
  • Keep breakable items on lower shelves
  • Know how to shut off gas, water, and electricity
  • Consider structural retrofitting if you live in an older home or high-risk area
PRO TIP: Store emergency supplies in multiple locations - at home, in your car, and at work. Check and rotate supplies every 6 months.

üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è During Earthquake: Immediate Action ‚ö†Ô∏è SHAKING!

GOLDEN RULE: DROP, COVER, AND HOLD ON! This is the single most important action to protect yourself during shaking.

üè† If You Are Indoors

  • DROP to your hands and knees immediately
  • COVER your head and neck with your arms
  • Take shelter under a sturdy table or desk if possible
  • HOLD ON to your shelter until shaking stops
  • Stay away from windows, glass, and exterior walls
  • DO NOT RUN OUTSIDE during shaking
  • If in bed, stay there and protect your head with a pillow
  • If no shelter available, crouch in an interior corner

üå≥ If You Are Outdoors

  • Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines
  • Drop to the ground to prevent being knocked down
  • Protect your head and neck with your arms
  • Stay low until shaking stops completely
  • Watch for falling debris from buildings
  • If near slopes, be alert for falling rocks and landslides

üöó If You Are Driving

  • Pull over to a clear location away from bridges, overpasses, and power lines
  • Set the parking brake and stay inside the vehicle
  • Turn on the radio for emergency information
  • Proceed carefully once shaking stops, watching for road damage
  • If a power line falls on your car, stay inside until trained personnel remove it

‚ôø If You Use a Wheelchair or Mobility Device

  • Lock your wheels and remain seated
  • Protect your head and neck with your arms, a pillow, or book
  • Move away from windows and hanging objects if possible

üö® After Earthquake: Recovery & Safety

‚ö° Immediate Actions

  • Check yourself and others for injuries - provide first aid if trained
  • Expect aftershocks - be ready to Drop, Cover, and Hold On again
  • Look for and extinguish small fires if safe to do so
  • Listen to battery-powered radio for official information
  • Use phones only for life-threatening emergencies - text messages use less bandwidth
  • Check for structural damage before re-entering buildings

üîç Safety Inspection

  • Gas leaks: If you smell gas or hear hissing, shut off main valve and evacuate
  • Electrical damage: Look for sparking wires - shut off power at main breaker if unsafe
  • Water pipes: Check for broken pipes and sewage lines
  • Hazardous materials: Clean up spilled chemicals, medicines, or cleaning products
  • Document damage: Take photographs for insurance claims

üìû If Trapped Under Debris

  • Stay calm and conserve energy
  • Tap on pipes or walls to help rescuers locate you
  • Use a whistle if available - shouting should be last resort
  • Cover your mouth with clothing to filter dust
  • Move carefully to avoid disturbing debris

ü§ù Community Response

  • Check on neighbors, especially elderly, disabled, or those living alone
  • Follow instructions from local authorities
  • Report hazards like downed power lines or gas leaks
  • Only enter damaged buildings when authorities declare them safe
  • Be prepared for potential evacuation orders
  • Participate in community clean-up when safe
REMEMBER: The time to prepare is NOW. Practice your earthquake safety plans regularly with all household members.
